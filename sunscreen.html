Benifits of wearing Sunscreen Everyday

Wearing sunscreen on your face and body is not only important for preventing sunburns, but it also provides many other benefits.

Protection from UV Rays

UV rays can penetrate the skin and damage the cells,
causing DNA mutations that may lead to cancer.
Regular use of sunscreen can help prevent these harmful effects by blocking or absorbing
the UV rays before they can penetrate the skin.

Prevents Sunburns

Sunburns occur when the skin is exposed to excessive UV radiation, causing redness, pain, and peeling.
Wearing sunscreen can help prevent sunburns by blocking or absorbing the UV radiation before it can damage the skin.

Reduces Skin Cancer Risk

The main cause of skin cancer is excessive exposure to UV radiation from the sun.
This is why it is important to wear sunscreen every day so you can reduce the risk of developing
skin cancer as it protects your skin from harmful UV rays.

How to Apply Sunscreen

Applying sunscreen correctly is crucial to ensure maximum protection from the sun’s harmful rays.
Here are some tips on how to apply sunscreen effectively:

  1. Apply sunscreen at least 15-30 minutes before going outside to allow it to fully absorb into the skin.
  2. Use enough sunscreen to cover all exposed skin. A general rule of thumb is to use about
    one ounce (or a shot glass full) of sunscreen for the entire body.
  3. Reapply sunscreen every two hours or immediately after swimming or sweating.
  4. Don’t forget to apply sunscreen to often overlooked areas such as the ears,
    back of the neck, and tops of the feet.
  5. Use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of at least 30 to protect against both UVA and UVB rays.
  6. Consider using a separate facial sunscreen with a higher SPF to protect the delicate skin on your face.

Tips for Using Sunscreen Effectively

  1. Apply sunscreen generously: Most people do not apply enough sunscreen, which reduces its effectiveness.
    To ensure maximum protection, apply a generous amount of sunscreen to all
    exposed areas of your skin.
  2. Reapply sunscreen frequently: Sunscreen wears off over time, especially if you are sweating or swimming. To maintain protection, reapply sunscreen every two hours or after swimming or sweating.
